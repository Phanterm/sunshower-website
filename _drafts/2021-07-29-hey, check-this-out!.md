<p>Hey everyone, time for a little blog post action -- literally!</p>

<p>Today I wanted to go into the nitty gritty of how I've designed combat in Coruncopia, from mindset to practical execution, to how it all scales over the course of the game. To begin with, our game is a little unique in that the main character starts out with the maximum amount of life possible, and becomes weaker as the game progresses. There are no opportunities to get more life, and enemies will nonetheless continue to become more powerful in the later parts of the game. This gives us a multitude of interesting design challenges:</p>

<ul class="numbered">
	<li>How do we make the player feel like they've made progress?</li>
	<li>How can we keep enemies balanced while also keeping them interesting to engage with?</li>
	<li>How will this mechanic of "losing" instead of "gaining" lead to more fun?</li>
</ul>

<p>Let's start from the top, shall we?</p>

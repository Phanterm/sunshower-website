Howdy there!

Keeping people in the loop has never been easier, but it's also never been more complicated. Instead of taking the usual approach of posting on each and every social media outlet to keep engaged, we decided to take a different approach. And so, we're happy to finally unveil a little something that's been in the works for a few weeks now. 

<h2>Engage This</h2>

This blog is more than just an obligatory place to let people know that we still have a pulse. Utilizing the power of <a href="https://jekyllrb.com/">Jekyll</a> and <a href="https://www.integromat.com/">Integromat</a>, our social media engagement will largely become automated, sourced from new blog posts that these services will watch, and then post links on the appropriate platform. Doing this allows us as developers to spend more time working on the games.

"But wait", you might be thinking. "How will you engage with your community?" That's an excellent question. The best place to reach us will always be our <a href="https://discord.gg/9jrXWTjvVf">Discord server</a>. As development ramps up, we may explore other options such as development livestreams, YouTube vlogs, or anything that works for us! While it's important to have a community presence, we also don't have a dedicated community manager on our small team, and need to prioritize our tasks accordingly without stretching ourselves thin. We're in this for the long-haul; it's crucial that we manage the scope of our organization in order to function effectively and efficiently.

Speaking of social media...

These aren't the only tasks in the air being juggled. There's a living game design document I'm updating constantly with trickles of new information about the game's construction that serve as a reference or design guide. I'm also communicating regularly with other members of the team to try and line everything up. Logistics, logistics, logistics! 

<h2>What's Next?</h2>

Besides what I mentioned above, I plan to use this blog to not only give updates on the game, but as a place to write down my thoughts about game design from time to time as they relate to Sunshower projects. I sincerely hope you enjoy reading them! In the meantime, I'll continue working on the main website during my free time -- we're nearly there, but still need a little more time before it'll be ready to unveil. Until then, I'll see you guys next time!

Oh, I nearly forgot. Our deadline for the vertical slice is <b>the end of Q4 2022</b> -- come hell or high water.
